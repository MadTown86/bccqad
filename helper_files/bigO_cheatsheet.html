üìù Important Notes:

  • Average vs Worst Case: Many structures show different performance under different conditions
  • Amortized Analysis: Some operations may be expensive occasionally but cheap on average
  • Space Complexity: Often there's a trade-off between time and space efficiency
  • Implementation Details: Real-world performance can vary based on specific implementation choices
  • Cache Performance: Arrays often perform better in practice due to better cache locality
  • Load Factor: Hash table performance heavily depends on maintaining a good load factor
  • Balancing: Self-balancing trees guarantee O(log n) performance but have overhead
